Careers






Singita’s success is built on the collective strength and vision of deeply committed people, all passionate about Africa and linked by a common purpose to protect and preserve the world’s last remaining wilderness areas through conservation, community development and hospitality.




Working with purpose

Pursuing excellence in your choice of career

Working in a fully empowered way

To work for Singita is to be part of something meaningful, far reaching and so much bigger than oneself. It’s an opportunity to work for a world-renowned brand, pursuing excellence in one’s area of expertise, including but not limited to guiding, guest relations, cheffing, IT, finance, human resources, community development and management in its various forms.

At our lodges across Africa, our staff live and work surrounded by nature, far from the usual challenges associated with modern life, including daily commuting to work and finding a suitable place to live!

The advantages of being in the bush also include life-changing opportunities to interact with an extraordinary range of influential people – our guests – who come from all around the world.

Staff at Singita Management Company in Cape Town and Johannesburg, as well as in the Arusha office, work in a dedicated way to support the reserve-based lodges. Everyone is part of an extended family, and no matter what their role, each is essential to Singita’s bigger picture.

Lodge Manager - Sasakwa Lodge, Singita Serengeti

Purpose

The main purpose of the Lodge Manager is to manage the lodge in a way that creates a profound guest experience, in line with the service vision and creative concepts of the lodge.

It is the responsibility of the Lodge Manager to monitor hospitality service, maintenance, gardening and housekeeping standards within the lodge. The role is additionally strategic in nature, and the Lodge Manager must drive the appropriate service directives for the lodges, and move the department towards achieving broader goals.

In this capacity, guest expectations regarding the standard of service should be exceeded. In addition, the lodges need to be monitored so that they run in a cost effective manner, within the agreed budget.

The Lodge Manager is also responsible for ensuring that the morale of the lodge staff is kept at a high level, that staff are developed on an ongoing basis and that staff with competencies and skills meeting job requirements are recruited for the department.

Scope of work

In broad terms the Sasakwa Lodge Manager oversees the following key performance areas:

  1. Drive the broader goals of the company, in terms of the lodge, by planning and implementing Strategic Management Plans.
  2. Management and training of the lodge staff in line with Singita standards.
  3. Develop staff to ensure successful succession.
  4. Oversee Sasakwa Lodge & Villa, Serengeti House, Sales Pavillion & Sabayaya at an operational level and ensure that the standards are consistent throughout the lodges and/or departments.
  5. Ensure the personal attention to guests’ are maintained at the correct standard.
  6. Maintain the highest standards of housekeeping and maintenance.
  7. Effective financial management through the administration of orders and effective stock control.
  8. Effective communication is maintained throughout the lodge and in each department.
  9. Maintain the design and look of the property as per the standards set out by the design team.
  10. Adapt and re-invent service standards according to industry demands.
  11. Bridge the gap between the lodge staff and property operational requirements and the expectations of the management company.

As such the person filling this position needs to have:

  1. A minimum of an advanced diploma in hospitality management, or a bachelor’s degree in business management, or a minimum of 6 year’s work experience in a well-recognized five star hotel or world class lodge, four of which must be in managerial position.
  2. A master’s degree or postgraduate diploma will be added advantage.
  3. Exceptional Food and Beverage knowledge.
  4. Financial management qualification and/or experience preferable.
  5. Leadership skills with a qualification and/or experience in communication and/or leadership.
  6. A hardworking, co-operative manner.
  7. High standards of service excellence and a passion for the industry.
  8. Exceptional attention to detail.
  9. Exceptional English and a second European language would be preferable.
  10. Good computer literacy and hotel management systems training.
  11. Excellent management ability.
  12. A clear understanding of basic labour law and disciplinary procedures.
  13. A developmental approach to staff.
  14. Assertiveness, patience and good organizational skills.
  15. Understanding of housekeeping and maintenance procedures.
  16. An awareness of developments within the food, bar and lodge industries, as well as international trends in hospitality.

Highly competitive remuneration package, commensurate with qualifications and experience, will be offered to the successful candidate.
